Introduction

As a junior studying computer science and English with a concentration in creative writing, I am often asked about my seemingly opposite majors. To me, however, both fields are centered around thoughtful planning and creative building. For both disciplines, all the information I need is available; programming languages have APIs and the English language has dictionaries. The challenge, then, is how I use and put this information together to create something new. What methods should I use to make my program run correctly? What words can I string together to best tell my story?

In these aspects of planning and building, I have developed strong problem-solving skills. Ironically, I have developed these skills through the problems I have run into. In studying computer-science, I initially struggled. This only incentivized me to breakdown concepts into smaller subproblems and thinking how I can attack those. I now have a strong resilience to failure and problems and am somone who takes inivitative to learn new skills. I have designed and then programmed web art, a personal website, proxy web servers, and data analyzers. As a course assisant for Introduction to Computer Science I work to build community by helping students with their class work and supporting their blossoming interest in the field.

I am a candidate with strong problem-solving skills who is looking for a project-based, collaborative position in which I am working to help build a product or service. This website started as an idea to practice some coding during the summer (for more information, see the Projects tab) and has morphed into this website that I hope serves as a small portfolio to showcase some of my skills, creativity, and personality. All code on the site is original unless otherwise stated.
